 Germany's Gerhard Schroeder calls for radical reform of the EU's stability pact to grant countries more flexibility over their budget deficits . The current EU rules limit the size of a eurozone country's deficit to 3% of GDP . Changes to the pact are due to be agreed at an economic summit in March . Mr Schroeder's intervention comes ahead of a meeting of the 12 Eurozone finance ministers on Monday to discuss the pact's reforms . The issue will also be discussed at Tuesday's meeting of finance ministers of all 25 EU members . A number of EU finance ministers are believed to favour only limited changes to the eurozone's rules . The European Commission acknowledged last month that it would not impose sanctions on countries who break the rules . But several countries, including Germany, have breached the rules consistently since 2002 without facing punishment without facing penalties .
