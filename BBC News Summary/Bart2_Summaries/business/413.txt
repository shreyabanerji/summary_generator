 The European Commission has called a truce in its battle with France and Germany over breaching deficit limits . The move comes after the pair vowed to run their budget deficits below the EU cap in 2005 - for the first time in four years . Under EU rules, member countries must keep their deficits below 3% of GDP . The European People's Party (EPP) attacked the Commission for backing down from punitive action . Germany welcomed the decision, with finance minister Hans Eichel saying it showed that the EC recognised Germany's fiscal policy was "on the right track even amid very difficult economic conditions" The EU refused to comment on similar action against Greece which has also broken the 3% deficit ceiling . It will be the third year in a row that the two countries have broken the European Union's Stability and Growth Pact rules .
