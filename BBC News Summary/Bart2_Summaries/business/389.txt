 Bank of Alexandria to be sold sometime in 2005 . Move seen as evidence of a new commitment by the government to reduce the size of public sector . The government has not yet decided whether the sale will take the form of a public flotation . The announcement reinforces the hopes of investors and international financial bodies for a revival of Egypt's privatisation programme . About 190 state-run companies and facilities were sold off from the early 1990s to 1997 . The appointment of Mahmoud Mohieldin, a reform-minded technocrat, to the new post of investment minister in July was taken as a sign more sell-offs were on the way .
