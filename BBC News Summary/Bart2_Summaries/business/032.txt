 Sumitomo Mitsui Financial has withdrawn its takeover offer for rival bank UFJ Holdings . The move clears the way for it to conclude a 3 trillion yen ($29bn) deal with Mitsubishi . The deal would create the world's biggest bank with assets of $1.8 trillion . The exit ends the most high profile fight in Japanese bank history . Japanese banks are increasingly seeking alliances to boost profits . However, this is expected to be a formality as shareholders of the two firms still have to approve the deal .
