 Russia's reputation among investors has been hurt in recent months by heavy tax bills and asset seizures imposed on companies such as oil giant Yukos . S&P said solidity of government finances outweighed the risk . Russia is now a net creditor rather than a debtor . Russia's gold and foreign currency reserves of $119bn beat its foreign public debt of some $113bn . Fitch and Moody's have long since upped their rating of Russia's sovereign debt . But the agency made it clear that the improved rating did not mean that the risks were a thing of the past . The Yukos saga is the most high-profile of the political risks to be a key ratings constraint on Russia, writes S&p credit analyst Helena Hessel . The ratings agency said that they now outweight the serious and growing political risk that continues to be an important rating constraint on the Russian government's own debt and said it would not necessarily be an investment decision .
