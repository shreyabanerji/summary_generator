 Venezuela reviews foreign deals with foreign firms to see if they provide maximum benefits to the country . Review will cover production of gold, aluminium and iron ore although it excludes the country's oil sector . President Hugo Chavez has sought to extend the state's role in all sectors of the economy . The left-wing president is conducting a controversial review of land ownership in the country while also seeking to create a state-run telecoms firm to compete with foreign-owned businesses . Companies from the United States, Canada, France and Switzerland all have substantial investments in Venezuela's mining sector . However, the government insisted that it needed to develop its own industrial infrastructure in order to create new jobs and lessen its reliance on foreign partners . The review of raw material production was announced by minister Victor Alvarez . He said: 'If we don't do this, we are just going to carry on being slaves ... and we will never develop our own productive capacity. We will never recover from being slaves, suppliers of raw materials'
