 China already has more than enough cigarette-making capacity, says tobacco industry regulator . Ban threatens to reignite tensions between regulator and British American Tobacco, which plans to become China's first foreign cigarette maker . China has 350 million smokers who consumer 1.7 trillion cigarettes a year . Smoking is fashionable in China, where it is seen as an essential - and manly - sociable touch for some jobs such as salesmen and salesmen . The state would retain its monopoly on cigarette distribution, according to a spokesman for the State Tobacco Administration Monopoly quoted in China Daily . Decision to ban further tobacco factories does not apply to deals made before 2005, says AFP . China Daily: The market was enjoying steady growth, making more than 210bn yuan ($25.4bn) in pre-tax profits last year, almost double the figure in 2000, but made no mention of health concerns.
