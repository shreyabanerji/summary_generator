 Finance ministers of the 12 eurozone countries voiced their concern that the rise of the european currency was harming exports . The dollar is within touching distance of an all-time low reached earlier in November . Nicolas Sarkozy of France said he and his colleagues were unanimous in their worry that the decline of the dollar would hit Europe's economies by eating into their exports . US Treasury Secretary John Snow said a strong dollar was "in America's interest" But that was not enough for Mr Sarkozy, who said the US needed to change its policy, to do so . Economists point out that in the short term a weaker dollar is a boon to President George W Bush's administration . But it also makes the budget deficit easier to fund. On the other hand, slower European exports would mean slower EU growth - potentially reducing the demand for U.S. goods . It rallied briefly on Monday amid signs that oil prices are easing.
