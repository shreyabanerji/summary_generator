 Growth for the whole of 2004 was 1.6%, after a year of contraction in 2003 . Germany reliant on exports to get its economy back on track . Unemployment of more than five million and impending cuts to welfare mean German consumers have kept their money to themselves . Rising exports outweighed in the fourth quarter by the continuing weakness of domestic demand . Eurozone interest rates are at 2%, but senior officials at the rate-setting European Central Bank are beginning to talk about the threat of inflation, prompting fears that interest rates may rise .
