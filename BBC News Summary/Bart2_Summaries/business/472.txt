 China suspends 26 power projects that failed to do proper environmental assessments . Topping the list was a controversial dam on the scenic upper Yangtze River . Government has encouraged construction of new electricity generating capacity to solve chronic energy shortages . China's energy shortage influenced global prices for oil, coal and shipping last year . In 2004, China increased its generating capacity by 12.6% or 440,700 megawatts (MW) The bulk of the list is made up of new power plants, with some extensions to existing ones . The stoppages would appear to be another step in the central government's battle to control projects licensed by local officials . However, previous crackdowns have tended to focus on projects for which the government argued there was overcapacity, such as steel and cement, like steel and other industries such as cement . In April 2004, Chinese officials ordered a halt to work on the nearby Nu River, which is part of a United Nations world heritage site, according to UK-published China Review .
