 Institute for Supply Management's index of national manufacturing activity rose to 58.6 last month from 57.8 in November . The result for December was slightly better than analysts' expectations and the 19th consecutive expansion . The ISM said the growth was driven by a "significant" rise in the new orders . The index has now been above 50 since June 2003 and is now above 50 for the longest time in a row in the history of manufacturing . The employment index eased to 52.7 in December, while its "prices paid" index also eased to 72.0 .
