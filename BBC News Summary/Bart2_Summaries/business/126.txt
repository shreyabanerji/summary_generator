 Earnings at Barclays Capital investment bank rose 25% to £1.04bn . But investment in branch operations held back growth in its UK retail business . Barclays chief John Varley said the bank had "caught the winds" of a very strong world economy . Group is first of Britain's five big banks to report 2004 results . HSBC, the biggest UK bank by stock market valuation, will report profits of £9.4bn later this month . Barclays shares in Barclays were trading down 0.67% at 590 pence on Monday morning after the results were in line with market expectations . Barclays cautioned that growth this year may be slower than in 2004 on the back of softer US and Chinese economies and the impact of interest rate rises on household spending in the UK . It added its bid to acquire a controlling stake in South Africa's leading retail bank Absa, was being considered by regulatory authorities. At 1405 GMT, shares were trading on Monday afternoon after the news of the results
