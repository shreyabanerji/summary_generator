 Businesses fail to draw up plans to cope with HIV/Aids until it affects 20% of people in a country . 71% of companies have no policies in place to address the disease, according to a report by the WEF and Harvard . The issue will be highlighted to business and world leaders at the World Economic Forum, which meets in Davos, Switzerland, next week . The UN programme tackling Aids, UNAIDS, pointed out that having a clear strategy for dealing with Aids was a good investment as well as being socially responsible . The gap is even wider in China, Ethiopia, India, Nigeria and Russia, the so-called "next wave" countries predicted to experience the highest numbers of new HIV cases worldwide by 2010 . The report concludes that businesses need to understand their exposure to HIV risks and come up with good local practices to manage them.
