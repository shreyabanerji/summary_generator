 US retail sales fell 0.3% in January, the biggest monthly decline since last August . Excluding the car sector, sales were up 0.6%, twice what some analysts had been expecting . Retail spending is expected to rise in 2005, but not as quickly as in 2004 . Sales at clothing and clothing accessory stores jumped 1.8%, while sales at general merchandise stores rose by 0.9% . In December, overall retail sales rose by 1.1%, while excluding car sales rose just 0.2% .
