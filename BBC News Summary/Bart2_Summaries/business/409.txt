 UK-based bank Standard Chartered said it would spend $3.3bn (£1.8bn) to buy one of South Korea's main retail banks . Bank raised funds for the deal by selling new stocks worth £1bn ($1.9bn) Shares fell nearly 3% in London as the bank raised funds by selling 10% of its share capital . South Korean banking market is three times the size of Hong Kong's with annual revenues of $44bn . Move would be South Korean financial sector's biggest foreign takeover of foreign banking sector . KFB is S Korea's seventh largest bank, with 3 million retail customers, 6% of the country's banking market and extensive branch network . The South Korean government owns 51.4% of KFB, while the remaining shareholding, and operational control, are in the hands of US private equity group Newbridge Capital . The deal is expected to be completed by April 2005 and to be earnings accretive in 2006, Standard .
