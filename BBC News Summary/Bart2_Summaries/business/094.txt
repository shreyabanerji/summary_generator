 US economy grew at an annual rate of 3.8% in the last quarter of 2004 . The rise reflects stronger spending by businesses on capital equipment and a smaller-than-expected trade deficit . The positive economic climate may lead to a rise in interest rates, with many expecting US rates to rise on 22 March . In the January-to-March quarter, the economy is expected to grow at a rate of about 4%, economists forecast . Consumer spending grew 4.2%, down from the third quarter's 5.1% growth .
