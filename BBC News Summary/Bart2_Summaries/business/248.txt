 Average house price in England and Wales was £182,920, down from £187,971 in July-September . Volume of sales between October and December dropped by nearly a quarter from the same period in 2003 . Biggest annual gains were made in Wales, where house prices were up by 23% in the fourth quarter of 2004 . House prices rose the slowest in Greater London, being up by 6% . Bank of England's rate-setting committee will make their latest decision on interest rates on Thursday . Although the figures point to a slowdown in the market, the most recent surveys from Nationwide and Halifax have indicated the market may be undergoing a revival. After registering falls at the back end of 2004, Halifax said house prices rose by 0.8% in January and Nationwide reported a rise of 0.4 percent in the first month of the year in January.
