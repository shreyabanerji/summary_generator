 George Soros' Open Society Institute says demand for unpaid taxes and fines is politically motivated . The organisation has found itself in trouble after being accused of helping to topple Georgia's former president . It denies having any role, but offices have had to close across the region . The OSI aims to promote democratic and open, market-based societies . Since the break up of the Soviet Union in 1991, Kazakhstan has been dominated by its president . He has powers for life, while insulting the president and officials has been made a criminal offence .
