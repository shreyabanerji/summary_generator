 Standard Life cuts policy bonuses for with-profit policyholders . Annual bonus rates on with-profits life policies were cut from 2.5% to 2%, while bonuses on pension policies were reduced from 3.25% . It is the sixth time in three years Standard Life has made cuts to bonus rates . The insurer blamed the cuts on poor share returns and low interest rates . Last year, the company announced that it was looking to float on the stock market in 2006 . It has an estimated 2.4 million policyholders, including 2.2 million with-profits policyholders and 2.7% rise in 2004 value of the fund in the past three years . In 2001 and 2002, Standard Life came in for criticism for sticking with stock market investments during 2001 and . 2002. The insurer argued that shares outperformed other investments over the long term and that policyholders would feel the benefit when the stock markets recovered.
