 157,000 new jobs were added last month, taking 2004's total to 2.2 million . Job creation was one of last year's main concerns for the US economy . The unemployment rate stayed at 5.4% in December, and about 200,000 jobs will need to be created each month if that figure is to drop . The Federal Reserve is likely to continue its policy of raising interest rates, analysts say . Consumers' debt has dropped by $8.7bn in November, but economists warn against reading too much into data from the Federal Reserve showing an unexpected $8bn drop in consumer debt in November . Analysts say there could be a number of reasons for a fall in the borrowing, which include credit cards and personal loans, while noting that such figures can vary on a month-to-month basis of the figures can differ from month- to month to month-on-month differences .
