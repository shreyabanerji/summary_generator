 Market unfazed by Aurora setback, says Deutsche Bank's Simon Champion . Analyst: Aurora issue had been an ongoing one for some time . But the stock market is very good at treating these issues as one-off events . Analysts expect the impact on the rest of the cruise business to be limited . With Carnival remaining the market leader, it looks set to keep selling the tickets - no matter what happens to the Aurora in the future, analysts say . In the wake of P&O Princess's takeover by Carnival, the business is now to a great extent a duopoly. Given the expense of building, outfitting and running a cruise ship, "slowing supply growth" is a certainty, said Merrill Lynch's David Anders at Merrill Lynch on Thursday. In other words, if you do want a cruise, your options are limited. And with Carnival remains the market lead, it will continue to sell the tickets, says Merrill Lynch.
