 UK Coal blames geological problems, industrial action and "operating flaws" at its deep mines for worsening fortunes . South Yorkshire company hopes to return to profit in 2006 . UK Coal operates 15 deep and surface mines across Nottinghamshire, Derbyshire, Leicestershire, Yorkshire, West Midlands, Northumberland and Durham . Recent rise in coal prices has failed to benefit company as most of its output had already been sold, it said . Company says it is making "significant progress" in shaking up the business .
