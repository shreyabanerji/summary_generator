 Stanley Fischer, vice chairman of Citigroup, will have to become an Israeli citizen to take the job . Previous incumbent David Klein, who often argued with the Finance Ministry, steps down on 16 January . Mr Fischer will face a delicate balancing act - both in political and economic terms - between Prime Minister Ariel Sharon and finance minister Binyamin Netanyahu . The appointment has also raised hopes that it could bring in fresh investment - and perhaps even an improvement in the country's credit rating - in a bid to attract fresh investment .
