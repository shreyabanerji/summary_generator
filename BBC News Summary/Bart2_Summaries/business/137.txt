 Bank set to leave rates on hold at 4.75% following latest meeting of the Bank of England . Rate-setting committee has put up rates five times in the past year but rates have been on hold since September . Economic growth slowed in the previous quarter, as manufacturing output fell, while consumer confidence has slipped . House prices fell 0.4% in October, their biggest monthly fall since February 2001, according to the Nationwide, their largest monthly fall in February 2001 . Richard Jeffrey, chief economist at Bridgewell Securities, said it was very unlikely that the Bank would put rates up again this time around .
