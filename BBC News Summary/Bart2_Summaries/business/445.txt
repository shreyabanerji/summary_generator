 Chief executive Willie Walsh, chief financial officer Brian Dunne and chief operations officer Seamus Kearney set to leave Aer Lingus early on 28 January . They announced in November they would quit in May, but did not give a reason . Mr Walsh denied they were forced out early because of reports they were set to launch a competitor airline . The company is 85% owned by the government and 15% by its staff . Irish Transport Minister Martin Cullen said a conflict of interest cannot, should not and will not be allowed to arise between their current roles and their future career intentions . He said he had received more than 40 business proposals, mostly aviation-related, since the trio announced their resignations two months ago and had been focused on his responsibilities . The airline is now 85%owned by the Irish government and is a low-fare airline, but has been under attack from Ryanair and its own staff and its budget budget budget has not yet been approved by its own employees and its staff members .
