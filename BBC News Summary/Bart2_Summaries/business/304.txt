 Paul Tellier, part of the family that controls Bombardier, leaves company amid restructuring . Laurent Beaudoin will take on the role of CEO under a newly created management structure . Analysts say the resignations seem to have stemmed from a boardroom dispute . Shares in the firm lost 65 Canadian cents or 25% on the news to 1.90 Canadian dollars before rallying to 2.20 Canadian dollars . Mr Tellier had indicated he wanted to stay at the firm until restructuring was complete .
