 Argentine water firm Aguas Argentinas wants a tariff rise of 60% to fund water-supply improvements . Planning Minister Julio De Vido has offered State help but not for 'free' He has said a contribution could be made in return for a seat on the company's board . Suez owns 40.9% of the firm, while Spain's Aguas de Barcelona is its second biggest shareholder with 25.01% . Recently, Suez lost a water concession in Bolivia after mass protests in the city of El Alto (the poorest in the country), with citizens complaining of unfair water charges . President Kirchner under pressure from European Union countries and the International Monetary Fund to raise utilities tariffs, because most of the utilities operating in Argentina are European . He has rejected any tariff increases, alleging this will impoverish citizens further. But he has also asked for more investments to meet the growing demand for water.
