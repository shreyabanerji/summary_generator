 Dollar hovers around record lows against the euro on Friday . Concern grows about the size of the US budget deficit . Bush administration's apparent unwillingness to intervene to support the dollar has caused further concern . Analysts predict that the dollar will remain weak in 2005 as investors worry about the state of the economy . German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder and Italian Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi voiced concerns about the strength of the euro . Investors will now look towards February's meeting of finance ministers from the G7 nations in London for clues as to whether central banks will combine forces to stem the dollar's decline .
