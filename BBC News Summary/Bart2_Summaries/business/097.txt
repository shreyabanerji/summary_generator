 US Treasury Secretary John Snow said a strong dollar was "in America's interest" But analysts said any gains are likely to be short-lived as problems with the US economy are still significant . A weak dollar helps boost exports and narrow the current account deficit . Policy makers in Europe have called the dollar's slide "brutal" and blamed the strength of the euro for dampening economic growth . Some analysts maintain the US is secretly happy with a lower dollar which helps makes its exports cheaper in Europe, thus boosting its economy . The dollar was trading at $1.2944 against the euro at 2100GMT, still close to the $1 .3006 record level set on 10 November . It was trading against the Japanese yen at 105.28 yen, after hitting a seven-month low of 105.17 earlier in the day . It is unclear whether ministers would issue a declaration aimed at curbing the euro's rise at a monthly meeting of Eurozone ministers late on Monday on Monday .
