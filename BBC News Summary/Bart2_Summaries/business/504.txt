A $2bn "funding gap" still stands in the way of hitting the 2005 target, WHO says . More than 20 other world leaders expected to attend including French President Jacques Chirac, South African President Thabo Mbeki and Ukraine's new president Viktor Yushchenko . Anti-globalisation campaigners have called off a demonstration planned for the weekend at the World Social Forum in Porto Alegre, the so-called 'anti-Davos' for campaigners against globalisation, for fair trade, and many other causes, but protests against the WEF are expected to be muted . The WEF is dominated by business issues - from outsourcing to corporate leadership - with bosses of more than a fifth of the world's 500 largest companies scheduled to attend . But the political discussions, focusing on Iran, Iraq and China, are likely to dominate media attention .
