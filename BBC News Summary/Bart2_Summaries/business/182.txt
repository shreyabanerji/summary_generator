 Official figures showed unemployment was unchanged at 9.9% last month, while consumer confidence fell unexpectedly in October . Nicolas Sarkozy warned that high oil prices posed a threat to French growth . The French statistics agency, INSEE, expects the economy to grow by about 2.4% this year, buoyed by strong consumer spending and business investment . Many economists have urged the government to stimulate employment by reducing non-wage payroll costs, and by scrapping restrictions on working hours . In France, it costs more to hire staff in France than in many other European economies .
