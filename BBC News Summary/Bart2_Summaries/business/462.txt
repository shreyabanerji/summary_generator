 World's biggest car maker is grappling with losses in its European business, and weak US sales . GM said it expects to meet 2004 earnings targets "despite a tough competitive environment" Car maker is in midst of cutting 12,000 jobs - one fifth of the European total - in a bid to cut costs . GMAC has been a strong contributor to profits in 2004 but GM says it will do less well this year, delivering net income of $2.5bn . The car maker has diversified into financial services, and is extending the reach of General Motors Acceptance Corp .
