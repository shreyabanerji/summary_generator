 Net income for 2004 was $3.5bn (£1.87bn) - up nearly $3bn from 2003 - while turnover rose $7.2bn to $170.8bn . Fourth quarter turnover was $44.7bn, compared to $45.9bn a year ago . But its auto unit made a pre-tax loss of $470m in the fourth quarter (compared to a profit of £13m in year-ago period) and its US sales dipped 3.8% . However, Ford is working hard to revitalise its product portfolio, unveiling the Fusion and Zephyr models at the International Motor Show in Detroit in Detroit . It also brought out a number of new models in the second half of 2004 . But Ford declined to provide guidance for first quarter 2005 at a presentation in New York on 26 January. It will do so at the New Year's Update for the first quarter of 2005 at 9.30pm ET on January 26 .
