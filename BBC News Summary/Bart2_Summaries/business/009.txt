 Good rains, increased use of fertilizers and improved seeds contributed to the rise in production . About 80% of Ethiopians depend directly or indirectly on agriculture . Agriculture is the main economic activity in Ethiopia, representing 45% of gross domestic product . A prolonged drought has killed crops and drained wells in eastern and southern Ethiopia, a prolonged drought . The Food and Agriculture Organisation (FAO) recommend that the food assistance is bought locally. The report calculated emergency food requirements for 2005 to be 387,500 tonnes .
