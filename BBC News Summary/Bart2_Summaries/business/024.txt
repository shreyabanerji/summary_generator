 US trade gap hits record in 2004 at $671.7bn (£484bn) in 2004, latest figures show . US consumer's appetite for all things from oil to imported cars, and even wine and cheese, reached record levels last year . The deficit with China, up 30.5% at $162bn, was the largest ever recorded with a single country . However, on a monthly basis the trade gap narrowed by 4.9% in December to £56.4bn . Democrats claim the administration has not done enough to clamp down on unfair foreign trade practices . Meanwhile, the Bush administration argues that the US deficit reflects the fact the America is growing at faster rate than the rest of the world, spurring on more demand for imported goods . Some economists say the deficit may allow an upward revision of US economic growth in the fourth quarter of the year . But others point out that the deficit has reached such astronomical proportions that foreigners choose not to hold as many dollar-denominated assets
