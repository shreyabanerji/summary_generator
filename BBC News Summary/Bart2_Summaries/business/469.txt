 IMF agrees fresh Turkey funding with the International Monetary Fund . Turkey's current $18.6bn loan agreement with the IMF expires in February . In return for the funding, Turkey would be expected to keep inflation under control and introduce market reforms . Turkey has a huge debt burden - already owing $23bn to the IMF - while its current account deficit has swelled to $10.7bn this year . The agreement must still be ratified by IMF directors at a meeting expected to take place next month . The EU will decide on 17 December whether to begin entry talks with Turkey . The US, the largest of the IMF's 184 members, is a strong supporter of continued financial support for Turkey. The IMF's managing director Rodrigo de Rato said the loan agreement would help to improve Turkish economic prospects by cutting its debt and stimulating growth.
