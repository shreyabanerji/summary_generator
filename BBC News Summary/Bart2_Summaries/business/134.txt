 337,000 new jobs added to US payrolls in October was twice the 169,000 Wall Street economists had forecast . Jobs were created in every sector of the US economy except manufacturing . Unemployment rate goes up to 5.5% from 5.4% in September, but this is because more people are now actively seeking work . The better than expected jobs data had an immediate upward effect on stocks in New York, with the main Dow Jones index gaining 45.4 points to 10,360 by late morning trading . The dollar rose temporarily on the job creation news before falling back to a new record low against the euro . There is also speculation that President Bush will deliberately try to keep the dollar low in order to assist a growth in exports, or to help the US's record trade deficit .
