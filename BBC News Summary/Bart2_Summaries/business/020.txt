 Call centre users 'lose patience' as the number of calls to call centres is growing at a rate of 20% every year . Call centres also saw a sharp increase of customers simply abandoning calls . One possible reason for the lack in patience is the fact that more customers are calling 'on the move' using their mobile phones . The average induction time for a call centre worker fell last year from 36 to just 21 days, leaving "agents not equipped to deal with customers" Poor training frustrates both call centre workers and customers, says Dimension Data . Nearly two-thirds of all firms polled for the survey have no plans to offshore their call centres . Only 42% of them say that saving money is the main consideration when closing domestic call centre operations . Half of them argue that workers in other countries offer better skills for the money.
