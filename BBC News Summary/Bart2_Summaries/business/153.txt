 Annual rate of increase in the UK is down to 10.2%, the lowest rate since June 2001 . Mortgage approvals fell in January to a near 10-year low, official Bank of England figures show . Average UK property now stands at £152,879, according to Nationwide . Homeowners now expect house prices to rise by 1% over the next six months . PWC warned house prices were overvalued and could fall by between 10% and 15% by 2009, PWC said last summer . New mortgage loans in January fell to 79,000 from 82,000 in December, the bank said . Overall, mortgage lending rose by £7.2bn in January, marginally up on the £8.1bn rise in December . The price of an average UK property is now at £16,000 per square square square foot, Nationwide said.
