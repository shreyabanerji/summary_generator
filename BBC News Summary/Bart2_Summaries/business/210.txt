 The price of gold fell to $413.50 an ounce in Asia, before rebounding slightly in early European trading . G7 finance ministers backed plans to write off up to 100% of the debts owed by some of the world's poorest countries . The US, which can veto any plan to sell IMF gold, said it is looking at other ways of solving the problem . The IMF has 3,217 tonnes of gold, or about 113.5m ounces, worth about 113m ounces . It values its gold reserves at between $40 and $50 per ounce, a price that was fixed in the 1970s and is about a tenth of the metal's current market value . But not everyone is convinced that selling IMF gold is the best way to solve the issue of debt relief is best way forward, with Canada, a key gold producer, also expressing reservations . The U.S. Treasury Under Secretary John Taylor says it is not convinced that that's the necessary way to do it .
