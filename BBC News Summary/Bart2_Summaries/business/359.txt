 Meeting between Fiat boss Sergio Marchionne and GM's Rick Wagoner is due to take place at 1330 GMT in Zurich . Both companies want to cut costs as the car industry adjusts to waning demand . Fiat claims that GM is legally obliged to buy the 90% of the car unit it does not already own; GM says the contract, signed in 2000, is no longer valid . Analysts say any legal battle would be protracted and damaging to the business . Political and public pressure may well push the two firms to reach a compromise, say analysts . The Agnelli family, which indirectly controls at least 30% of Fiat, has not given a firm public indication that it wants to sell the auto business, analysts say . It is so closely linked to Italy's industrial heritage that it may not be easy to sell its car-making unit, analysts said .
