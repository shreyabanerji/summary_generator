 Japan's long economic downturn helped drive the trend as drinkers looked for cheaper alternatives to drown their sorrows . Market for so-called "beer-like" drinks is set to grow 84% this year . Asahi Breweries predicts profits to rise 50% in 2005 as it launches a drink based on soybean peptides rather than malt . Suntory, Sainory and Sapporo are already well-established with beer-flavoured "Draft One" and happoshu . Kirin is launching its own "third type" drink in April .
