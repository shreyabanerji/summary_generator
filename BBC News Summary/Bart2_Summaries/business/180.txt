 US dollar drops against major currencies on concerns that central banks may cut the amount of dollars they hold in their foreign reserves . Comments by South Korea's central bank sparked the recent round of dollar declines . South Korea plans to boost holdings of currencies such as the Australian and Canadian dollar . Analysts reckon that other nations may follow suit and now ditch the dollar . At 1300 GMT, the euro was up 0.9% on the day at 1.3187 euros per US dollar . The British pound had added 0.5% to break through the $1.90 level, while the dollar had fallen by 1 .3% against the Japanese yen to trade at 104.16 yen . The focus once again has been on the country's massive trade and budget deficits, with predictions of more dollar weakness to come, analysts say . At the start of the year, the US currency had staged something of a recovery. But analysts say that many of the U.S. economic problems had not disappeared.
