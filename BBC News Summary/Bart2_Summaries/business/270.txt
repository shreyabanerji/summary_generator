 Brent crude closed at $40.15 on Thursday - a drop of 5.1% . US light crude traded in New York lost more than $2 to $43.25, its lowest close since 10 September . Price of crude is still up about 30% on the start of 2004, but has fallen from the record of $55.67 set in late October . Opec nations have increased production to 25-year highs to meet global demand . However, traders warn that the fall could be short-lived if there is a cold snap in North America this winter or any major supply problems in other parts of the world . A cold spell in north America would start to deplete supplies and could spark further price rises . Analysts say prices will fall further if inventories continue to rise in the next few weeks .
