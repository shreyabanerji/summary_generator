 Arsenal vice-chairman David Dein says the club may consider seeking a full listing for its shares on the London Stock Exchange . The club is due to move into its new 60,000-seater Emirates Stadium at Ashburton Grove for the start of the 2006/07 season . Mr Dein also warned the current level of TV coverage of the Premiership may be reaching saturation level . The Gunners are looking to increase matchday earnings from 29% to 40% of turnover, and have not ruled out other money-earning means, including a full share listing, he says . Arsenal is looking to create new revenue streams from overseas markets, including Asia, where it has two executives travelling round Japan and China at the moment building relationships with organisations and clubs, as they are growing there too, as well as debt from banks, and from the sale of surplus property, including its Highbury Stadium .
