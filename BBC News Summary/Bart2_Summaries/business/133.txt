 About 500 officials are accused of either awarding themselves extra money or claiming salaries for "non-existent" workers . Prime Minister Ephraim Inoni vowed to tackle corruption when he came to office last year . He said those found guilty would face tough punishments . The scam is believed to have begun in 1994 and is thought to have cost Cameroon $2m a month . In 2003, a report compiled by Transparency International said graft was said to be "pervasive" in Cameroon . In one initiative, civil servants who arrived late for work were locked out of their offices .
