 Bank opts to leave rates on hold at 4.75% for a sixth month in a row . Some economists predict a further rise in the cost of borrowing will come later this year . Inflation, however, has been rising faster than expected, hitting an annual rate of 1.6% in December - its highest level in six months . Business groups welcome the MPC's widely-expected move and caution against further rises . The British Chambers of Commerce said it was "concerned by the clamour in some quarters" for early interest rate increases . The CBI said it is "firmly resisted" by the demand for early rate rises. The Bank of England governor Mervyn King has recently warned against placing too much weight on one month's economic data, suggesting the MPc is waiting for a clearer picture to emerge. The MPC faces an "interesting" challenge. Inflation is expected to rise again this year. The inflation report is due to be released next week.
