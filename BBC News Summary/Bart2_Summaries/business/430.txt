 Sales of hybrid cars in the US are set to double in 2005, research suggests . Toyota has led the market to date with the Prius, popularised by celebrities keen to burnish their "green" credentials . Toyota will launch a hybrid version of its Highlander SUV, with an SUV from its luxury Lexus marque due later in the year . The rise in oil prices over the past year has sparked hopes that consumers may be tempted by potential savings of a few hundred dollars a year on fuel . Some watchers point out that the price tag on a hybrid - upwards of $3,000 above that of an equivalent normal-engined car, and suspicion of the technology - may still cool its attraction . Others remain sceptical about the hybrid technology, with some saying it may still be a popularity in the U.S. It will be difficult to find a buyer for such a hybrid car to drive more than six years to buy a new model .
