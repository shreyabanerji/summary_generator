 Russian oil merger excludes Yukos-owned Yugansk, which was controversially bought by Rosneft last year . Gazprom chief Alexei Miller confirms Yugansk will not be part of the deal and will instead be spun off . The state wanted to control Gazprom before allowing foreigners to trade in Gazprom shares . The deal should go through in the next two to three months, according to reports . Analysts say the deal would give Gazprom control of 8% of Russia's total oil production, but still less than the 20% share it would have gained had it also taken over Yugansk . The merged group will rank alongside Sibneft with 7% of the market, but will still remain outside Russia's top five oil producers - led by Lukoil with 11%, followed by TNK-BP which is half owned by BP, and Surgutneftegaz.
