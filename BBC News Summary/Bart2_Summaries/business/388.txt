 A further 40,000 farms are yet to be inspected, the state's National Land Institute says . Critics of land reform claim president Hugo Chavez is trying to enforce a communist-style economic programme that ignores property rights . The government - Venezuela's largest land owner - say they are proceeding cautiously to prevent conflicts . One property in conflict with the government is the El Charcote cattle ranch, run by Agroflora, a subsidiary of the UK food group Vestey . The UK government has asked Venezuelan authorities to resolve the conflict .
