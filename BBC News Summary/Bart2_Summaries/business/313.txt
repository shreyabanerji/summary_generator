 Irish oil company Petrel Resources lost more than 50% of its value on a report that the firm has failed to win a contract in Iraq . Reuters news agency reported that Iraq's Oil Ministry has awarded the first post-war oilfield contracts to a Canadian and a Turkish company . Petrel said that it has not received any information from Iraqi authorities to confirm or deny the report . Iraq is still trying to boost its production capacity to match levels last seen in the eighties before the war with Iran . Oil officials hope to double Iraq's output by the end of the decade .
