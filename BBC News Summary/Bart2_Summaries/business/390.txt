 Lawsuit alleges Wal-Mart committed thousands of gun sales violations in California between 2000 and 2003 . Total payment includes $5m in fines and more than $4m to fund state compliance checks with gun laws . The world's largest retailer has not yet decided whether to resume firearms sales in the state . The company has suspended firearm sales in its California stores in 2003, but has yet to decide whether it will resume sales in state in the next few years, a spokesman says . The settlement will ensure that it follows state law if it renews sales in California .
