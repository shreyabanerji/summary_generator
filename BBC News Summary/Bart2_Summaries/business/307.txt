 Cairn Energy shares closed down 18% after disappointing drilling update and warning over possible tax demands . Indian government has told company to pay production tax, which Cairsn argues it is not liable . Chief executive Bill Gammell said he was "disappointed" with exploration in the so-called N-C extension area in Rajasthan . Investors had held high hopes of major oil finds in this area, but company said estimates had been revised in what was a 'significant downgrade of the initial expectation' Cairns' shares have jumped by almost 400% this year after announcement of significant oil find in India this year . Company said the tax would wipe 5% of the field's current value, but said it would only become an issue once the firm started production . The shares close down 247p, or 18%, at 1115 pence. The company was catapulted into the FTSE 100 index of leading UK shares earlier this year with the sharp rise in its share price
