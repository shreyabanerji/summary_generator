 ISEQ index of leading shares closed up 23 points to 6661.89 on Thursday . A fall in the rate of inflation to 2.3% in January gave a fresh boost to shares which have advanced 4% this month . The economy is set for strong growth in 2005 while interest rates remain low . Analysts say Irish shares are still trading at a discount to other European markets . Ireland's economic 'miracle' is enjoying a second wind, with growth of just under 5% expected in 2004, with 5% growth forecast for 2005 and 2006 . The country's biggest company by capitalisation touched a five year peak while Bank of Ireland shares rose to their highest level since August 2002 .Telecoms firm Eircom, which recently revealed that it would re-enter the Irish mobile phone market, hit a yearly high, hit its yearly high in the market last week . The Irish economy cooled markedly between 2001 and 2003 after enjoying spectacular growth of more than 10% in 2000 .
