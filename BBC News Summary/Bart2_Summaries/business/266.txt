 Study argues that more rigorous tax policing has benefited all Russian firms, even targets of the tax police . The study's reasoning is complex, and is based on a sophisticated model of the relationship between tax regimes and corporate governance - in particular, the propensity of management to steal from the company . But increasing the rigour with which taxes are collected encourages companies to become more transparent, forcing them to be able to demonstrate their financial position far more accurately . The extra amount companies pay in tax is more than compensated for by greater efficiency and financial soundness, the authors say .
