 The European Central Bank has left its key interest rate unchanged at 2% for the 19th month in succession . High oil prices, meanwhile, have put upward pressure on the inflation rate . The strength of the euro is threatening to dampen foreign demand in 2005, and domestic consumption is not strong enough to take up the slack . Inflation in the eurozone is estimated at about 2.3% in December, above ECB guidelines of 2%. ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet has remained upbeat about prospects for the region .
