 UK economy probably grew at a faster rate in the third quarter than the 0.4% reported, says Bank of England deputy governor Rachel Lomax . Private sector business surveys suggest a stronger economy than official estimates, she says . Base rates will probably stay on hold at 4.75% despite strong growth, BoE says . House of Lords Economic Affairs Committee said the MPC had held interest rates too high given inflation was way below the 2% target . The BoE is working in tandem with the ONS to improve the quality and speed of delivery of data, she said . Improving the quality of national statistics is the best way of making sure the Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) makes the right decisions, she added . The clearest signals come when all indicators are pointing the same direction, Ms Lomx said .
