 French economy picked up speed at the end of 2004, official figures show . Growth for the three months to December was a seasonally-adjusted 0.7-0.8%, ahead of the 0.6% forecast . If confirmed, that would be the best quarterly showing since early 2002 . GDP up 2.3% for the full year, but short of the government's 2.5% target for 2005 . Service sector activity improved in January, hitting a seven-month high . But unemployment remains high at about 10%.
