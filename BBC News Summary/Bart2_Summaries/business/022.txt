 Unemployment rose 7,000 in November to 4.464 million people, or 10.8% of the workforce . Seasonally adjusted rise showed a smaller rise than expected, as government measures to encourage job creation began to take effect . The brunt of the unemployment is still being felt in the eastern part of Germany, where the rate is 18.8%. With unemployment stuck above 4 million for years, the government of Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder has put job creation at the top of the agenda . A controversial package of measures to shake up incentives to get back to work, paid for by cutting some cherished benefits, has sparked anger among some German workers . Strikes in a number of industries, notably among the country's iconic carmakers, have demonstrated the displeasure - as well as fears about further job losses as outsourcing takes hold .
