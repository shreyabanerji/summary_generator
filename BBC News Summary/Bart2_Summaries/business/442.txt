 Six foreign-owned textile factories have closed in Lesotho, leaving 6,650 garment workers jobless . The closures blamed the closures on the end of worldwide textile quotas . The quotas for developing nations, ended on 1 January, gave them a set share of the rich countries' markets . The owners - two from Taiwan, two from China, one from Mauritius and one from Malaysia - left over the December holiday period without informing or paying their employees . It is feared as many as 50,000 textile workers could lose their jobs, and it is feared more companies will leave the country . The assistance of a US law had given Lesothotho's textiles duty-free access to North American markets. The African Growth and Opportunity Act (AGOA) gave sub-Saharan countries preferential access to the US market for apparel and textile products as well as a wide range of other goods .
