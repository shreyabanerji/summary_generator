 US sales at GM sank 12.7% in February compared to a year ago . Ford sales dropped 3% as foreign rivals took a bigger share of the market . Asian carmakers fared well - Toyota sales jumped 11% while rival Nissan notched up a 10% increase . Overall US sales across the industry also fell to 1.25 million vehicles from 1.27 million a year earlier . GM and Ford blamed high fuel prices for low sales of big trucks and gas-guzzling SUVs - the vehicles that provide the biggest profits . Chrysler, the US unit of Germany's DaimlerChrysler, was the only Detroit based automaker to boast an increase in market share during the month - with sales rising 8% on the month . Toyota put its rise in sales down to strong results for its redesigned Avalon sedan and a 120% surge in sales of its gas-electric Prius hybrid mid-size sedan in a statement . It has already reduced output by around 9% .
