 BA makes pre-tax profit of £75m ($141m) compared with £125m a year earlier . Fuel costs rose by £106m or 47.3% in the third quarter . BA's profits were still better than market expectation of £59m . BA last year introduced a fuel surcharge for passengers to help offset the increased price of aviation fuel . BA also reported passenger numbers rose 8.1% in January following the 11 September 2001 attacks in the U.S. BA has cut 13,000 jobs as part of a major cost-cutting drive since 9/11 attacks in U.K. BA chairman Martin Broughton had previously forecast a 2% to 3% rise in full-year revenue rise in 2004 .
