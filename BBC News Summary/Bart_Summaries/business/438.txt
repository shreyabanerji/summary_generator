UK interest rates held at 4.75% in widely-predicted move. Recent data has indicated a slowdown in manufacturing and consumer spending. Retail sales disappointed over Christmas, with analysts putting the drop down to less consumer confidence. Some economists have predicted rates will drop later in the year, although others feel the Bank may still think there is a need for a rise to 5% before that happens. The Bank remains concerned about long-term risks posed by personal debt - which is rising at 15% a year.
