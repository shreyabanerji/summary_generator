Asia quake increases poverty risk. Paris Club of rich creditor nations on Wednesday offered to freeze debts owed by tsunami-hit countries. Donor nations have promised to give $717m in disaster relief over the next six months, according to the United Nations. There are 1.9 billion people in Asia living on less than $2 a day. The ADB fears that 1 million Indonesians could join them, while in India just over half a million people - 645,000 - are at risk of falling into poverty.
