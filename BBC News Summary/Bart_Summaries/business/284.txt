Economy has become the second-most dominant issue for many political parties. Job creation ranks high both on election manifestos and on the Iraqi people's wish list. Some progress has been made, largely thanks to the country's oil revenues which have exceeded $22bn since June 2003. But serious problems remain and the growing divide between haves and have-nots is angering voters.. Foreign banks have been buying into the sector, but few are actually entering the country in any meaningful way. In contrast with the daily plight of ordinary people, 19 private banks operate, only one of which is run in accordance with Islamic banking principles.
