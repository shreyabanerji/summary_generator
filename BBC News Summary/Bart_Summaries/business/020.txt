Call centre users 'lose patience' and hang up after just 65 seconds of listening to canned music. Number of calls to call centres is growing at a rate of 20% every year. Problems occurring because increased responsibility is not going hand-in-hand with more training, survey finds. Nearly two-thirds of all firms polled for the survey have no plans to offshore their call centres. The average induction time for a call centre worker fell last year from 36 to just 21 days, leaving "agents not equipped to deal with customers"
