SEC to rethink post-Enron rules to ease burden on foreign firms. William Donaldson promised "several initiatives" in speech to London School of Economics. European firms have protested that US laws make Wall Street listings too costly. US regulator said foreign firms may get extra time to comply with a key clause in the Sarbanes-Oxley Act. It obliges all firms with US stock market listings to make declarations, which critics say will add substantially to the cost of preparing annual accounts.
