Industrial output falls in Japan

Japanese industrial output fell in October while unemployment rose, casting further doubt on the strength of the country's economic recovery. Although inventories fell 0.7% month on month, they were 36% higher than a year ago. " It's a sign that the economy's adjustment phase is stronger than expected," said Takashi Yamanaka, an economist with UFJ Bank. Japanese government officials played down the latest data, arguing that domestic consumer demand was still resilient. "
