Asia quake increases poverty risk

Nearly two million people across Asia could be thrown into poverty because of the Indian Ocean tsunami, the Asian Development Bank (ADB) has said. But the blow to many low-income people could be "enormous". In the Maldives, where 43% of the population already lives on less than $2 a day, this could rise to half. However, it remains too soon to asses the damage to poor people's livelihoods in Aceh because it would depend on how much farm land had been flooded by seawater.
