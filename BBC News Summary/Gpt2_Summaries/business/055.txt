SA unveils 'more for all' budget

The South African government has put tax cuts and increased social spending at the centre of its latest budget. Spending on health and education will rise by 9.4% and 8.1% respectively. All the spending increases will run over the next three years. Unveiling the 418bn-rand budget to parliament, Mr Manuel said the South African economy had grown by an average of 3.2% over the past four years, slightly below the African average of 4%.
