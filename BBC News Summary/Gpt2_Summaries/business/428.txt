Karachi stocks hit historic high

The Karachi Stock Exchange (KSE) has recorded its largest single day gain, surging 3.5% to a new high. The KSE index closed at 6709.93 - an overall gain of nearly 400 points in the first two trading days of the week. Market analysts are inclined to agree with Mr Lakhani, arguing that there are a number of major factors behind the KSE's performance. One leading Karachi broker said the real reasons may be political. " Most importantly, say analysts, the failure of talks between India and Pakistan on the Baglihar dam in Indian-administered Kashmir has not automatically led to heightened tensions. You can call it a peace dividend," smiles one broker. "
