Turkey knocks six zeros off lira

Turkey is to relaunch its currency on Saturday, knocking six zeros off the lira in the hope of boosting trade and powering its growing economy. Inflation has since been tamed and economic prospects are improving. On an everyday level, it is hoped the change will stimulate more international trade and end confusion among foreign investors and Turks alike. " The transition to the new Turkish lira shows clearly that our economy has broken the vicious circle that it was imprisoned in for long years," said Sureyya Serdengecti, head of the Turkish Central Bank. " The slimmed-down lira is likely to be widely welcomed by the business community.
