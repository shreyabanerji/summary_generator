MCI shares climb on takeover bid

Shares in US phone company MCI have risen on speculation that it is in takeover talks. Other firms have also expressed an interest in MCI, the second-largest US long-distance phone firm, and may now table rival bids, analysts said. Shares in MCI, which changed its name from Worldcom when it emerged from bankruptcy, were up 2.4% at $20.15. It hit the headlines as Worldcom in 2002 after admitting it illegally booked expenses and inflated profits.
